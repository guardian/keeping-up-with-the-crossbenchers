Keeping up with the crossbenchers

The disintegration of Pauline Hanson’s One Nation comes after a turbulent 23 months for the Senate. Politicians have switched parties multiple times, quit politics and fallen victim to the dual citizenship crisis. Here’s how it all happened
Nick Evershed
Scroll down

After the 2016 double-dissolution election, the new Senate crossbench is made up of four One Nation senators, three from the Nick Xenophon Team, senators Jacqui Lambie, David Leyonhjelm and Derryn Hinch, and Family First's Bob Day.

November 2016: Family First's Bob Day resigns when his company goes into liquidation. He was later found to not have been eligible for election due to an issue under section 44 of the constitution. One Nation's Rod Culleton is referred to the high court and found not to have been validly elected owing to a conviction for larceny which was subsequently annulled but had stood throughout the election period.

February 2017: Cory Bernardi quits the Liberal party and creates his own party, the Australian Conservatives.

March 2017: The vacancy from One Nation's Rod Culleton is filled by his brother-in-law, Peter Georgiou.

April 2017: The Family First vacancy is filled by Lucy Gichuhi. She subsequently becomes an independent as Family First dissolves.

October 2017: Malcolm Roberts is found ineligible to have been elected because of his dual citizenship. Nick Xenophon, who was also referred to the high court, announces his intention to resign from the Senate to run for the South Australian state parliament. Xenophon is later found to have been eligible for election and so could have remained in the Senate had he chosen to do so.

November 2017: Malcolm Roberts' vacancy is filled by One Nation's Fraser Anning. Nick Xenophon's vacancy is filled by Rex Patrick.

November 2017: Jacqui Lambie and Skye Kakoschke-Moore, a Nick Xenophon Team senator, both resign from the Senate owing to dual citizenship issues.

January 2018: Fraser Anning declares he is leaving One Nation and will sit as an independent senator.

February 2018: Steve Martin fills the Jacqui Lambie Network vacancy left by Lambie's resignation, then announces he has left the party and will be sitting as an independent

February 2018: Lucy Gichuhi joins the Liberal party, saying: “I didn’t discover that until I did my maiden speech and I thought: 'Wow – I can’t believe how Liberal I am at the core.'”

February 2018: Tim Storer fills the Nick Xenophon Team vacancy left by Kakoschke-Moore, then 10 days later declares he has left the party and will be sitting as an independent

April 2018: The Nick Xenophon Team rebrands as the Centre Alliance

May 2018: Former Jacqui Lambie Network and then independent senator Steve Martin announces he has joined the Nationals

June 2018: Pauline Hanson accuses fellow One Nation senator Brian Burston of wanting to join the Shooters and Fishers party, which Burston denies. Burston remains a One Nation senator (for the moment).

June 2018: Former One Nation and then independent senator Fraser Anning announces he has joined the Katter party, saying Katter’s Australia party represents traditional Australian values and a commitment to infrastructure development.

June 2018: One Nation senator Brian Burston quits the party and announces he will sit as an independent. Burston tells Fairfax: “There is no democracy in the party – every single decision made is made by Pauline Hanson, and if you don’t agree then you’re gone.”

June 2018: Former One Nation and then independent senator Brian Burston joins Clive Palmer's as-yet-unregistered United Australia party
